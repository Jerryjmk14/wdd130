Nasarawa State, Nigeria

Nasarawa State is located in the central region of Nigeria, often referred to as the Middle Belt. It was created on October 1, 1996, from the western half of Plateau State. The state is known for its diverse ethnic groups, including the Alago, Eggon, and Mada, among others. Agriculture is a significant part of the economy, with crops like yams, maize, and rice being commonly cultivated. Nasarawa is also rich in mineral resources such as tin, columbite, and limestone. The state capital is Lafia, which serves as the administrative and economic hub.
